1. Why are the variables declared as private?

The variables are declared as private to protect the student's information.
They cannot be accessed or changed directly from another class.

2. What happens if the variables are declared public?

If the variables are public, any class can access and change their values
directly. This can make the data unsafe or incorrect.

3. Why do we use getters and setters?

Getters are used to read private variables, while setters are used to change
their values safely. They provide controlled access to the student's data.
